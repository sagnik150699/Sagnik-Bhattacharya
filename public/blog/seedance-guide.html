Seedance 2.0: The Complete Guide

The complete Seedance 2.0 hub — setup, prompts, settings, camera movement, consistent characters, use cases, and model comparisons.

Seedance 2.0 is one of the most capable AI video models available in 2026, and this hub collects every Seedance tutorial on the site. It covers first-time setup, pricing, core settings (resolution, motion intensity, export formats), prompt techniques, use-case-specific workflows (marketing videos, YouTube Shorts, product ads, anime, talking heads), and head-to-head comparisons against Sora 2, Kling, and Veo 3.

Who This Guide Is For

If you are brand new to AI video generation, start with the Complete Beginner's Guide — it walks through every step from account creation to your first generated video. Then read the Pricing and Credits post so you understand the credit economics before you generate at scale. If you are an experienced AI video user who has used Sora, Runway, or Kling and is evaluating Seedance specifically, jump straight to the Prompt Techniques and the comparison posts at the bottom — they explain exactly where Seedance excels (prompt adherence, camera control, character consistency) and where it falls behind. If you are a content creator or marketer producing video for a specific format, go directly to the Use Cases section — each tutorial covers a single output type (marketing ads, YouTube Shorts, product videos, anime) with prompts and settings tuned for that format.

How to Navigate This Guide

The 20 tutorials below are organised into six sections. Getting Started covers everything from setup to your first generation. Settings and Technical Controls explains the parameters that most affect output quality — resolution, aspect ratio, export format, and the motion intensity slider (which is the single most important setting after the prompt itself). Prompt Techniques covers the prompt structure patterns that produce reliable output, including camera movement vocabularly and audio-driven generation. Use Cases provides format-specific workflows — each post gives you ready-to-use prompts and settings for a common video type. Consistency and Troubleshooting addresses the two pain points that frustrate everyone: maintaining character identity across clips and diagnosing motion artefacts. Comparisons tests Seedance head-to-head against the main alternatives.

The Prompt-First Workflow

The single most important thing to understand about Seedance 2.0 is that it weights the first 15 words of your prompt disproportionately. Every tutorial on this site follows the same structure: subject first (what is in the frame), then motion (what is happening), then camera (how the camera moves), then mood and style (lighting, colour grade, aesthetic). If you write prompts in any other order, you will get inconsistent results. The prompt techniques tutorial covers this in detail, but keep it in mind as you read any of the use-case guides — they all follow this pattern.

For best results, generate at draft resolution first (720p, shorter duration), iterate the prompt until the content matches what you envisioned, and only then re-render at full quality. This saves credits and avoids the frustration of waiting for a high-resolution render that does not match your intent.

Every tutorial is written from hands-on testing inside the Dreamina interface — not generic AI video advice. The workflows are the ones that have produced reliable, repeatable output in real content pipelines.

Key takeaways

  • Seedance 2.0 weights the earlier parts of a prompt more heavily — put subject, motion, and camera direction in the first 15 words.
  • Motion intensity 40-60 produces natural movement for most scenes; under 30 reads as static, over 75 introduces warping and artefacts.
  • For consistent characters across multiple clips, reference images beat text description alone — it is the single biggest reliability fix.
  • Seedance outperforms Sora 2 on prompt adherence but trails on raw photorealism; it outperforms Kling on camera movement fidelity.
  • Generate at draft resolution first, iterate the prompt, then re-render at full quality — it saves credits and time.
  • The Dreamina interface is the primary way to access Seedance 2.0 — there is no standalone desktop app or CLI.
